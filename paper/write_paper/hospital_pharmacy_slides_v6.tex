\section{County-level Results}

\begin{frame}{County-level Empirical Setting}

\small
\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{0.65em}
    \item Unit of analysis: county-by-year panel, 2012--2022.
    \item Main controlled sample: 2012--2019, with population, nighttime lights, and road-density controls.
    \item Main treatment indicator:
    \[
        Reform_{ct}=\mathbf{1}\{t\geq PolicyYear_c\}.
    \]
    \item Baseline FE specification:
    \[
        Y_{ct}=\beta_0+\beta_1 Reform_{ct}+\beta X_{ct}+\alpha_c+\lambda_t+\varepsilon_{ct}.
    \]
    \item Controls include log population, log nighttime lights, and log road density where available.
    \item Standard errors are clustered at the county level.
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

\section{Hospital-level Results}

\begin{frame}{Hospital-level DDD Specification}

\small
\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{0.7em}
    \item Unit of analysis: hospital-by-year panel with pharmacy outcomes measured within 1km, 2km, and 3km buffers.
    \item Treatment contrasts public hospitals with non-public hospitals after local reform implementation:
    \[
        Y_{ict}=\beta_0+\beta(Public_i\times Post_{ct})+\theta Post_{ct}
        +X_{ict}'\gamma+\alpha_i+\lambda_t+\varepsilon_{ict}.
    \]
    \item $\beta$ is the differential post-reform change around public hospitals.

    \item $\theta+\beta$ is the post-reform change around public hospitals relative to the pre-period.
    \item Controls include nighttime lights around hospitals where available. Standard errors are clustered at the county level.
    
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{How the County and Hospital Results Connect}

\scriptsize
\begin{itemize}\setlength\itemsep{0.7em}
    \item \textbf{County-level estimates show market expansion.}
    Pharmacy counts rise after reform in the county FE models, and annual count changes increase sharply.
    \item \textbf{Distance results do not imply broad movement away from hospitals.}
    Average distance and the within-2km share are almost unchanged, while counts within 2km rise.
    \item \textbf{Public-hospital proximity rises in absolute terms but weakens in relative terms.}
    Pharmacies within 2km of public hospitals increase, but the public-hospital share falls while the private-hospital share rises.
    \item \textbf{Hospital DDD is a relative comparison.}
    The negative Public $\times$ Post coefficient means public hospitals experience smaller growth than non-public hospitals, not necessarily an absolute decline around public hospitals.
    \item \textbf{Interpretation.}
    Reform appears to expand the county pharmacy market overall, with growth near hospitals, but new entry is relatively less concentrated around public hospitals than around private or non-public hospital areas.
\end{itemize}

\end{frame}
